In a shocking revelation, former employees of Gunzilla Games, a blockchain game development studio co-founded by acclaimed director Neill Blomkamp, have alleged that they have not been paid for several months. This situation raises critical questions about the treatment of artists and developers in the gaming industry, particularly in the emerging blockchain sector, where the allure of innovative technology often overshadows fundamental labor rights.

Paul Creamer, a former animator at Gunzilla, claims he has been unpaid since October 2025, highlighting a troubling pattern of delayed salaries that has affected multiple staff members. Despite assurances from upper management that these issues were merely growing pains, the reality appears to be far more severe, with employees feeling ignored and silenced when they seek answers about their compensation.

As the gaming industry continues to evolve, it is essential for professionals to advocate for their rights and ensure that such predatory practices do not become the norm. How can we as a community come together to support those affected and hold companies accountable for their commitments to their employees?
