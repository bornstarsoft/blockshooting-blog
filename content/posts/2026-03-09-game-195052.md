Cheongju City has recently reported a remarkable increase in its employment rate, reaching 66.0% in the second half of 2025, a 1.4 percentage point rise compared to the previous year. This achievement is particularly noteworthy as it positions Cheongju at the forefront of major cities with populations over 800,000, leading in OECD-standard employment metrics, including youth and female employment rates.

The city has successfully reduced its unemployment rate to just 1.9%, the lowest among major urban centers, thanks to strategic initiatives aimed at fostering job creation and supporting new growth industries. Cheongju's proactive approach, which includes building job collaboration networks and enhancing the business environment, serves as a compelling case study for other regions aiming to boost their economic performance.

As we look to the future, the question remains: how can other cities replicate Cheongju's success in creating sustainable job opportunities and fostering economic resilience? The journey towards a robust job market is ongoing, and Cheongjus experience offers valuable insights for cities around the globe.
